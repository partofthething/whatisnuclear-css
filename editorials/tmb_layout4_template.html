What is nuclear?

Learn about nuclear energy

It is one of the best options we have.

Every day, we're becoming more concerned about energy. Global warming, dependence on foreign oil, shortage of resources, and increasing population constantly top the headlines. Peaceful nuclear energy offers compelling solutions to many of these problems, but its public image is distorted at best. Here, we offer facts on both the good and bad aspects of nuclear energy, hoping to open up minds to this vast energy resource that is often disregarded.

Did you know

Standard nuclear waste is more than 90% Uranium. There is enough recoverable energy in the waste accumulated today in the US to power the country for over 200 years.

Why don't we use this resource? The recycling of fuel was shut down in the '70s due to proliferation concerns.

Today, new technology greatly reduces these concerns. Should we bury this resource or start recycling? Find out more.

Hydrogen production

Nuclear reactors use a ceramic fuel that can be operated at temperatures up to 1000 degrees C. At these temperatures, water separates into hydrogen and oxygen. The hydrogen can then be used in fuel cells to power cars.

So, while these reactors would be producing electricity, they could form hydrogen as a byproduct. Then we could have all our power without the greenhouse emissions.
