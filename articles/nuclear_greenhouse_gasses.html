Lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions of nuclear power plants

One of the huge advantages of nuclear plants over many others is that the energy-releasing nuclear reactions don’t produce greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane -- wastes that could be altering the climate. But certainly greenhouse gases are produced by the Uranium mining operations, the fuel enrichment process, the construction of the plant, and maintenance. Here, we collect and review 13 studies comparing the greenhouse gas emissions of several power sources and conclude that nuclear power plants produce something like 18 kg of CO2-equivalent for every MW-hour of electricity produced. Compared to 928 for coal, 439 for natural gas, 67 for solar PV, 18 for wind, and 11 for hydro, nuclear is clearly distinct from fossil fuels, competing with and sometimes outperforming the renewables.
